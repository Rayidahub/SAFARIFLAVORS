Ugali

Prep: 5 mins
Cook: 15 mins
Yields: 10 Servings
For Beginners

01.

Boil Water: Pour a measured amount of water into a saucepan or a cooking pot. The amount of water will depend on the quantity of ugali you want to prepare. As a general guideline, you'll use about 2 parts water to 1 part maize flour, but you can adjust this ratio based on your preference.

02.

Add Maize Flour: Once the water starts to boil, reduce the heat to low and gradually add the maize flour while stirring continuously. It's important to add the flour gradually and keep stirring to prevent lumps from forming.

03.

Stir and Cook: Continue to stir the mixture vigorously to ensure the maize flour is well incorporated into the water. You want to achieve a smooth, thick consistency. Keep cooking and stirring for about 5-10 minutes. The ugali should start pulling away from the sides of the pot and forming a cohesive mass.

04.

At this point, you can shape the ugali into a mound or any desired form. You can wet your hands with a little water to prevent sticking while shaping. Traditionally, ugali is often shaped into a dome or served in rounded portions. It's typically served as an accompaniment to stews, vegetables, or meats.That's it! You've successfully prepared Ugali. Remember, practice helps perfect the consistency, so don't worry if it's not perfect the first time. Adjust the water-to-flour ratio based on your preference for a softer or firmer texture. Enjoy your Ugali!
